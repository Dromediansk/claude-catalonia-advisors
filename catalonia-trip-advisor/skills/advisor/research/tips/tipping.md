# Tipping norms in Catalonia and Spain

**Tipping in Spain is not the US.** It is not expected, not built into wages, and not a fixed percentage. Service staff earn a proper salary and tips are a small, optional gesture for genuinely good service. Round numbers and small coins, not 15–20%.

## The headline rules

- **Not 15–20% like the US.** Repeat: not 15–20%. Tipping that amount is unusual and can be slightly embarrassing for the recipient.
- Rounding up the bill to the nearest euro or leaving small change is the everyday norm.
- **5–10% is generous**, reserved for nicer sit-down restaurants when service was good.
- **Cash, on the table.** Card terminals in Spain rarely prompt for a tip; if you tip on card the money may not reach the server. Leave euro coins or a small note.
- Tipping is never legally required and refusing to tip is not rude.

## Specific situations

### Bars and cafés

- Espresso, *cortado*, beer, *vermut* at the bar: **no tip needed**. Locals leave the small copper coins (1c, 2c, 5c) in the change dish, or nothing at all.
- A €1.50 coffee paid with a €2 coin: leaving the 50c is normal. Not leaving it is also normal.
- Tapas at a counter: round up to the nearest euro if you feel like it.

### Casual / mid-range restaurants (menú del día, neighbourhood lunch spots)

- Round up the bill, or leave **€1–€2 per person**.
- €38 bill → leave €40. That's a tip.

### Nicer sit-down restaurants & dinner out

- **5% is normal for good service. 10% is generous.** Anything more is unusual.
- €80 bill, good service → leave €85, or up to €88. Don't leave €96.
- Fine dining / Michelin: 5–10% if service warrants. Some high-end places include a service charge (**servei inclòs** / **servicio incluido**) — check the bill before adding more.

### Taxis

- Round up to the nearest euro. €13.40 fare → €14.
- For a long airport run with luggage help: €1–€2 extra is plenty.
- Cabify / Free Now / Uber: tipping in-app is optional and uncommon; locals rarely do.

### Hotels

- **Housekeeping:** €1–€2 per night, left on the pillow or with a note. End of stay or daily.
- **Bellhop / porter:** €1–€2 per bag.
- **Concierge:** €5–€10 for a hard-to-get restaurant booking or similar favour.
- **Doorman hailing a taxi:** small change or nothing.

### Tours and guides

- **Free walking tours (which aren't actually free):** **€5–€10 per person** is the expected tip if you enjoyed it. €15–€20 for a multi-hour deep-dive with a great guide.
- **Paid private tour guide:** **5–10%** of the tour cost, or **€10–€20** for a half-day group tour.
- **Tapas tour / food tour:** €5–€10 per person on top of the ticket price.

### Other services

- **Hairdresser / barber:** round up or €1–€2.
- **Spa / massage:** not customary; round up if you wish.
- **Food delivery (Glovo, Uber Eats):** €1 or in-app tip if you want.
- **Bar tender at a club:** not expected.
- **Tour bus driver:** not expected.

## How tipping works practically

- **Paying by card:** ask for the bill (**"el compte si us plau"**), the waiter brings the terminal, you tap/insert. The terminal almost never prompts for a tip. To tip, leave **cash on the table** as you leave.
- **Splitting bills:** completely normal. Ask "**podem pagar per separat?**" (can we pay separately?) or just hand over multiple cards — most places handle it without fuss.
- **Service charge:** rare in Barcelona but check the bill for *servei inclòs* (Catalan) or *servicio incluido* (Spanish) before tipping on top. A *cobert* (cover charge) is for bread/setting and is **not** a tip.
- **VAT (IVA):** already included in menu prices. You're not tipping on top of an untaxed amount.

## What not to do

- Don't tip 18–20%. It's not generous in Spain, it's just confusing.
- Don't apologise for leaving only €1 — it's appropriate.
- Don't tip at fast-food, supermarkets, or counter pickup. Nobody does.
- Don't feel pressured by US-style card terminals at touristy spots that prompt for 10/15/20% — those are designed for foreigners and locals ignore them.
