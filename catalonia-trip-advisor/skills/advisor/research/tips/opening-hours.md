# Opening hours and the Catalan daily rhythm

Barcelona runs on a later, more spread-out schedule than northern Europe or the US. Planning around it is the difference between eating dinner and being told the kitchen is closed.

## The daily rhythm

- **Breakfast (esmorzar / desayuno):** 08:00–10:00. Coffee + croissant or *entrepà* (sandwich) at a bar. Most cafés open from 07:30 or 08:00.
- **Almorzar / esmorzar de forquilla:** mid-morning second breakfast around 10:30–12:00. Small plate, beer or vermouth at a bar.
- **Vermut hour:** 12:00–14:00 Saturdays and Sundays especially. Vermouth + olives + small tapa before lunch.
- **Lunch (dinar / comida):** **13:30–16:00**. The main meal. Kitchens typically take last orders around 15:30. **Most non-touristy restaurants will not seat you for lunch after 15:30.** Best-value option is the *menú del día* — 3 courses + drink, typically **€13–€18** [verify] on weekdays.
- **Afternoon coffee (berenar / merienda):** 17:00–19:00. Sweet snack, coffee.
- **Vermut / aperitiu before dinner:** 19:30–21:00.
- **Dinner (sopar / cena):** **20:30–23:00**. Kitchens often don't open until 20:30 or 21:00 and take last orders around 22:30–23:00. Showing up at 19:00 hungry for dinner: tourist trap or you're waiting an hour.
- **Bars / nightlife:** late bars from 23:00, clubs from 01:00, peak crowd 02:00–05:00.

## Shop opening hours

### Small / independent shops (boutiques, bookshops, family stores)

- **Morning:** 10:00–14:00
- **Closed:** 14:00–17:00 (lunch + the so-called "siesta" — nobody actually naps, but shops shut)
- **Afternoon:** 17:00–20:30
- **Saturdays:** often morning only, or full day; many smaller shops close all Saturday afternoon.
- **Sundays:** **closed**.

### Larger shops & chains (Zara, El Corte Inglés, FNAC, etc.)

- **Continuous hours:** roughly **09:30/10:00 – 21:00/22:00** Monday–Saturday. No midday break.
- **El Corte Inglés (Plaça de Catalunya, Diagonal):** Mon–Sat **09:30–21:30** [verify].
- **Sundays:** usually closed, except on specific authorised opening Sundays (see below).

### Tourist-core shops (La Rambla, Passeig de Gràcia, Portal de l'Àngel)

- Open daily, often **10:00–22:00**, including Sundays. Different rules apply because central Barcelona is a designated tourist zone.

## Sunday & public holiday closures

- **Most shops:** closed.
- **Restaurants and cafés:** open. Sunday lunch is a major Catalan family tradition — book ahead.
- **Museums and tourist sights:** open. Sunday is a normal museum day in Barcelona.
- **Mercat de la Boqueria and most municipal markets:** closed Sunday. Most also close Sunday + Monday.
- **Mercat de Sant Antoni:** Sunday morning hosts a famous book/coin market (until ~14:00).
- **Authorised Sunday openings:** Catalonia designates 7 [verify] specific Sundays/holidays per year when shops can open. Falls around Christmas shopping, summer sales kick-off, and major bridge weekends.

## Supermarkets

Hours vary slightly by branch — check the door of your specific store.

- **Mercadona** — typical hours **Mon–Sat 09:00–21:30**. **Closed Sundays and most public holidays** [verify]. The default supermarket for most residents.
- **Bonpreu / Esclat** — typical hours **Mon–Sat 09:00–21:00 or 21:30**. **Closed Sundays** (except authorised openings) [verify]. Catalan-owned, slightly pricier than Mercadona, good quality.
- **Caprabo** — typical hours **Mon–Sat 09:00–21:30**. Some branches open Sunday 09:00–15:00 in tourist areas [verify].
- **Carrefour Express / Carrefour Market** — many central locations open **daily, including Sundays**, often **09:00–22:00** [verify]. Smaller, pricier than full supermarkets.
- **Lidl** — Mon–Sat **09:00–21:30** typically; closed Sundays outside authorised dates [verify].
- **Condis** — Mon–Sat **09:00–21:30**; some Sunday morning openings [verify].
- **Veritas** (organic) — Mon–Sat **09:00–21:30** typically [verify].

**Sunday food shopping rule of thumb:** Carrefour Express in central Barcelona is your reliable open-Sunday option. Otherwise look for small convenience shops (often run by Pakistani/Bangladeshi owners, locally called "*xinos*") which stay open 7 days a week including evenings — slightly pricier but a lifesaver.

## Public holidays (festius)

Spain mixes national, Catalan, and Barcelona-specific holidays. Major ones to know:

- **1 January** — Any Nou / Año Nuevo
- **6 January** — Reis / Reyes (Three Kings — the big gift-giving day, not Christmas)
- **Setmana Santa** (Holy Week, dates vary) — **Divendres Sant** (Good Friday) and **Dilluns de Pasqua** (Easter Monday) are holidays
- **1 May** — Festa del Treball (Labour Day)
- **24 June** — Sant Joan (Catalonia's bonfire night; the night before is wild)
- **15 August** — L'Assumpció (Assumption — peak summer holiday)
- **11 September** — Diada Nacional de Catalunya (Catalan National Day)
- **24 September** — La Mercè (Barcelona's patron saint festival — biggest local fiesta)
- **12 October** — Festa Nacional d'Espanya (Spanish National Day; politically charged in Catalonia)
- **1 November** — Tots Sants (All Saints)
- **6 December** — Constitució / **8 December** — Immaculada
- **25 December** — Nadal / **26 December** — Sant Esteve (Catalonia-specific second Christmas day)

On these days: shops closed, public transport on reduced schedule, museums often closed (especially 1 Jan, 25 Dec, sometimes 1 May), restaurants open but book ahead.

## August: the city half-shuts

- **Many family-run restaurants and shops close for 2–4 weeks** between mid-July and the end of August. Look for **"tancat per vacances"** ("closed for holidays") signs on doors.
- **Peak closure window:** roughly **1–25 August**, with **15 August** the highest point.
- Local-neighbourhood places (your favourite *forn*, *bodega*, *bar de barri*) most likely to close. Tourist-zone restaurants, chains, and big museums stay open.
- Check Google / Instagram for individual restaurants before walking across town in August.

## Sights — typical patterns

Always check the specific attraction's website for current hours, but expect:

- **Sagrada Família:** open daily ~09:00–20:00 in summer, ~09:00–18:00 in winter [verify]. Closed mornings of 25 Dec, 1 Jan, 6 Jan and 26 Dec [verify].
- **Park Güell (monumental zone):** open daily, timed-entry ticket required, ~09:30–19:30 [verify].
- **Most museums:** **closed Mondays** (MNAC, Picasso Museum, MACBA) — major exception.
- **Mercat de la Boqueria:** Mon–Sat ~08:00–20:30, closed Sunday [verify].

## Pharmacy hours

- Standard pharmacies (**farmàcies**): Mon–Fri 09:00–14:00 and 16:30–20:30 [verify]; Saturday mornings only.
- **24-hour rotation:** at least one pharmacy per district stays open 24h on rotation. The schedule is posted on every pharmacy door. Also check `farmaciesdeguardia.com` [verify] or call 010.
- **Always-open pharmacies (24/7, central):** Farmàcia Clapés on La Rambla 98 [verify], Farmàcia Cervera at Passeig de Gràcia 26 [verify].
