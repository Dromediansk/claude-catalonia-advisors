# Common tourist scams in Barcelona

Most "Barcelona scams" are distraction setups for pickpocketing rather than outright fraud. The pattern is almost always: one person gets your attention, another lifts your wallet/phone. Recognising the opening move is the whole defence.

## 1. Petition / clipboard scam

- **Where:** La Rambla, Plaça de Catalunya, around Sagrada Família, Park Güell entrances.
- **The setup:** Young person (often claiming to be deaf, mute, or collecting for "disabled children") approaches with a clipboard or, increasingly in 2025–2026, a **tablet**. Asks you to sign a petition.
- **The lift:** While you read/sign, an accomplice picks your pocket or bag. Or after signing they pressure you for a "donation" (€20+).
- **How to respond:** Don't stop. Don't engage. A firm "No, gràcies" and keep walking. If they follow, switch direction or step into a shop.

## 2. Fake "found gold ring"

- **Where:** Quieter side streets in Barri Gòtic, around tourist sights.
- **The setup:** Someone walks ahead of you, "discovers" a gold ring on the ground, picks it up and offers it to you — saying it must be yours or that you should share the luck.
- **The lift:** Once you handle/accept it, they demand money for "finder's fee" or as you reach for your wallet to refuse, an accomplice lifts it. The ring is brass, worth nothing.
- **How to respond:** Don't touch it. Keep walking. "No és meu" (it's not mine) and move on.

## 3. Bird-poop (mustard) distraction

- **Where:** Near tourist attractions, busy crossings.
- **The setup:** A stranger points out that a "bird" has pooped on your shoulder/back — actually mustard, mayo, or ice cream they sprayed. A "kind" passer-by helps you clean up.
- **The lift:** During the cleaning, your bag/wallet is gone. The helper and the bird-poop-pointer are working together.
- **How to respond:** If you feel something land on you, **don't take off your bag**. Walk to a shop or café first, then check. Refuse any stranger's help.

## 4. Friendship bracelet / sprig of rosemary

- **Where:** La Rambla (especially the lower half near the Mirador de Colom), outside Sagrada Família, Plaça Reial.
- **The setup:** Someone grabs your wrist warmly and begins braiding a coloured bracelet onto it before you can refuse. Or a woman presses a sprig of rosemary into your hand "for good luck" and reads your palm.
- **The sting:** They demand €10–€20 once it's on (the bracelet has to be cut off). The rosemary version: aggressive demand for payment plus possible pickpocket while you fumble for cash. **In 2025+ they target couples — approach the woman while the man is taking a photo.**
- **How to respond:** Hands in pockets when walking the Rambla. Pull your wrist away immediately. Never accept anything physically handed to you on the street. Walk away — they rarely follow far.

## 5. Fake police "checking for counterfeit notes"

- **Where:** Anywhere central, often Barri Gòtic.
- **The setup:** Two men in plain clothes flash a "police badge", say they're undercover, claim there's a counterfeit-money problem in the area and ask to "check" your wallet/passport.
- **The lift:** They palm cash or cards while inspecting the wallet, or simply walk off with it.
- **How to spot:** **Real Spanish police never ask to inspect your wallet on the street.** They wear uniforms (Mossos: blue + red trim; Guàrdia Urbana: navy blue; Policía Nacional: dark blue). Plain-clothes officers will not request your cash.
- **How to respond:** Refuse. Ask to see ID properly and say you'll walk with them to the nearest commissaria. They will leave. If unsure, call 112.

## 6. Airport taxi tricks

- **Where:** El Prat (BCN) — anyone approaching you inside the terminal offering a taxi is a tout, not a licensed driver.
- **The setup:** 
  - "Friendly" person inside the terminal offers a fixed price (€80–€150) to the city. Unlicensed.
  - Licensed driver from the rank offers a "flat rate" instead of the meter — always more expensive than the meter would be.
  - Driver claims the meter is broken.
  - Driver takes a long route via the Ronda Litoral when the direct route via Gran Via is faster.
  - "Extra" surcharges fabricated on arrival.
- **The honest fare:** Airport → city centre on the meter is roughly **€35–€45** including the airport supplement (€4.50 [verify]) and any luggage fee. **Minimum charge €21** [verify, 2025 tariff]. Cruise port flat rate is **€45** (officially set).
- **How to respond:**
  - Only use the **official black-and-yellow taxi rank** outside Terminals 1 and 2. Ignore anyone offering rides inside.
  - Insist on the meter (**"el taxímetre, si us plau"**).
  - Pay by card if possible — all licensed Barcelona taxis must accept cards.
  - Alternative: book via app — **Free Now, Cabify, PideTaxi**. Or take the **Aerobús** (€7.25 [verify], 35 min to Plaça de Catalunya) or **Rodalies R2 Nord** train (€4.90 [verify] with T-Casual).

## 7. ATM shoulder-surfing & card-trapping

- **Where:** Tourist-zone ATMs, especially **Euronet** (purple/yellow) machines on La Rambla and around Sagrada Família.
- **The setup:**
  - Someone stands too close behind you and watches your PIN, then a partner bumps you and lifts the card moments later.
  - Card-skimmers physically attached to the reader.
  - The Euronet machine itself: extortionate fees and a **Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC)** trap that costs 10–13% extra (see `money.md`).
- **How to respond:**
  - Use **bank-branded ATMs** inside or attached to a bank: **CaixaBank, BBVA, Banc Sabadell, Santander, Bankinter**. Avoid Euronet.
  - Shield the keypad with your other hand.
  - Tug the card slot gently before inserting — skimmers often wobble.
  - If a stranger offers to "help" with the machine, cancel and leave.

## 8. Restaurant menu without prices / dual menus

- **Where:** Terrace restaurants on La Rambla and immediate side streets (Carrer dels Escudellers, Plaça Reial), some spots near Sagrada Família and the beach.
- **The setup:**
  - No prices on the menu, or only on the chalkboard the waiter conveniently doesn't show.
  - A "tourist menu" handed to you while locals get a different one with lower prices.
  - "Specials" verbally pitched at 3–5× normal price (€80 paella, €18 sangria-by-the-glass).
  - Hidden cover charge (**cobert**), "bread" you didn't order, bottled water billed at €8.
  - Card terminal "broken" — cash only, no receipt.
- **How to respond:**
  - **Refuse to sit** if the menu has no prices. Walk away — there's a non-scam place 100m further.
  - Avoid restaurants on La Rambla itself. Two blocks east or west the same food costs half.
  - Ask the price of any "special" the waiter pitches verbally.
  - Check the bill line by line before paying. You can legally refuse charges for items you didn't order.
  - Always ask for a receipt (**rebut** / **factura**).
  - Rule of thumb: a paella for one in 25 minutes from a photo-menu on La Rambla is a microwaved frozen one. Real paella takes 30+ minutes and is for two minimum.

## 9. Other things to watch for

- **"Three-cup" / shell game** on La Rambla — entirely rigged, the "winners" are confederates. Walk past.
- **Top-up scams at metro vending machines** — strangers offering to "help" tap T-Casual onto your card take a few rides for themselves. Buy at the staffed window or use the official **TMB app**.
- **Fake event tickets** sold near Camp Nou, Palau de la Música, concert venues. Buy from official websites only.
- **"Free" hugs / photos with costumed characters** on La Rambla — they will demand €5–€10 after.

## If you get scammed

- Petty cash loss: write it off, move on, be more careful.
- Card stolen: freeze it immediately in your banking app, then file a *denúncia* with Mossos d'Esquadra at **Carrer Nou de la Rambla 76–80** (24/7, English available). The report is required for insurance claims.
- Passport stolen: Policía Nacional foreigners' office, **Passeig de Sant Joan 189**, then your embassy.
