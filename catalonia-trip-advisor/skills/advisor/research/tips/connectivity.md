# SIMs, eSIMs, WiFi, and essential apps

Barcelona has solid 4G/5G coverage citywide and reliable free WiFi on metro platforms, in libraries, parks, and council buildings. Getting a local data plan is cheap and easy.

## EU roaming first — check before buying

- If your home plan is **already in the EU/EEA** (any EU member, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway), **"Roam Like at Home"** applies — your domestic plan should work in Spain at no extra cost.
- **UK plans:** post-Brexit, many UK carriers reintroduced roaming fees. Check (EE, Vodafone UK, Three, O2 differ).
- **US / non-EU plans:** roaming usually expensive (T-Mobile is a partial exception). An eSIM is almost always cheaper.

## eSIMs (easiest for most travellers)

If your phone supports eSIM (iPhone XS or newer, Pixel 3 or newer, most recent Samsungs):

- **Airalo** — Spain-only or Europe-wide eSIMs. Spain plans from around **$5 / 1GB / 7 days** up to **$30+ / 20GB / 30 days** [verify]. Install before you land.
- **Holafly** — unlimited-data plans, slightly pricier. Around **€19 / 5 days, €27 / 7 days, €47 / 15 days** [verify]. No local number.
- **Saily** — newer entrant, competitive pricing.
- **Nomad** — Spain plans from around **$6 / 1GB** [verify].
- **Orange Holiday Europe eSIM** — **30GB / 14 days** around **€40** [verify], includes a real phone number and EU-wide coverage.
- **Ubigi, GigSky** — alternatives.

Install via QR code before departure. Some plans activate on first connection in Spain, others on a scheduled date — read the activation rules.

## Physical SIMs (if you prefer)

Buy from the carrier directly (need passport for registration):

- **Vodafone España** — large coverage, official stores throughout the city. Prepaid tourist packs around **€15–€30 / 20–50GB / 30 days** [verify].
- **Orange España** — similar. Look for "Mundo Sin Fronteras" or holiday packs.
- **Movistar** (Telefónica) — incumbent, slightly pricier, best rural coverage.
- **Yoigo** — budget operator on the Movistar network.
- **Lobster** — English-language customer service, popular with English-speaking expats. **€10–€20 / month** prepaid plans [verify].
- **Lowi, Simyo, MásMóvil** — MVNOs (cheaper, use the big networks). Often cheapest data per GB.

Stores at **El Prat T1** arrivals, Plaça de Catalunya, Passeig de Gràcia, Estació de Sants. SIMs typically **€10–€30** with included data.

## Free WiFi

- **Barcelona WiFi (Barcelona Wifi)** — the council-run free network. Look for the **"Barcelona Wifi"** SSID. Available across **hundreds of public spots**: civic centres, libraries, parks, museums, plaças, beaches, and select metro stations.
- **Metro stations** — **TMB WiFi** on platforms at major stations including **Catalunya, Universitat, Plaça d'Espanya, La Sagrera, Arc de Triomf, Sagrada Família, Sants Estació, Diagonal** and others [verify the full list]. Speed capped at **256 kbps** by law, so usable for messaging/maps but not video.
- **On buses:** TMB WiFi on most bus routes.
- **Airport (BCN)** — free WiFi throughout T1 and T2, no time limit.
- **Cafés:** most cafés and chains (**Costa**, **Starbucks**, smaller independents) offer free WiFi — ask for the password.
- **McDonald's / Burger King** — reliable open WiFi.

WiFi is generally good, but for navigation and ride-hailing while moving around, a local SIM/eSIM beats hopping between hotspots.

## Coverage notes

- **5G** is rolled out across central Barcelona on Vodafone, Orange, Movistar.
- **Metro underground** — patchy. Mobile data drops between stations on some lines; WiFi at platforms fills the gap.
- **Rural Catalonia / mountain areas (Montserrat, Pyrenees)** — Movistar generally has best coverage.

## Must-have apps

### Public transport

- **TMB App** (Transports Metropolitans de Barcelona) — official metro/bus app. Live arrivals, journey planner, ticket purchase (T-Casual, T-Usual, etc.), virtual T-Mobilitat card. **The single most useful Barcelona app.**
- **Citymapper** — Barcelona is covered. Best multi-modal journey planner combining metro, bus, FGC, Rodalies, walking, Bicing.
- **Moovit** — alternative public-transport app.
- **Renfe Cercanías / Rodalies de Catalunya** — for suburban and regional trains (airport R2 Nord, Sitges, Montserrat, Girona day trips). Buy tickets in-app.
- **Renfe** — long-distance / AVE high-speed trains (Madrid, Valencia, Seville).
- **Google Maps** — works well; **download Barcelona offline** before you arrive for fallback.

### Taxis / rides

- **Free Now** — works with regular Barcelona taxis (the black-and-yellow ones). The de-facto local taxi app.
- **Cabify** — private ride-hailing (Uber-style), often cheaper for set routes.
- **Uber** — operates in Barcelona but with restrictions (long pre-booking windows in some cases [verify]); less common than Cabify locally.
- **PideTaxi** — alternative taxi-hailing.

### Bikes / scooters

- **Bicing** — Barcelona's public bike-share. **Requires a Spanish address / NIE** to sign up — **mostly not accessible to tourists**.
- **Bolt** — e-scooters and e-bikes, tourist-accessible.
- **Donkey Republic** — bike rentals tourists can use.
- **Lime** — e-bikes and scooters in some zones [verify current operation in BCN].

### Food, daily life

- **TheFork (ElTenedor)** — restaurant reservations, often with discounts.
- **Glovo** — food / grocery / convenience delivery. Spanish unicorn, ubiquitous in Barcelona.
- **Uber Eats**, **Just Eat** — also operate.
- **Wallapop** — Catalan-born marketplace (second-hand).

### Tickets and culture

- **Sagrada Família** official app/site for timed entry.
- **Tiquets Museus de Barcelona** [verify name] — many museums via official ticketing.
- **Mosaic-of-Barcelona / Gaudir Més** — Catalan culture-pass info.

### Practical

- **Google Translate** — download Catalan and Spanish offline. Camera mode for menus.
- **Yuka** (food/cosmetic scanner) — popular in Spain for label reading.
- **AccuWeather / Meteocat** — Catalan met service, accurate local forecasts.

## Power & adapters

- **Sockets:** Type **F (Schuko)**, two round pins. Same as most of continental Europe.
- **Voltage:** **230V / 50Hz**.
- UK / US / Asia travellers: bring a Type F adapter. Most modern phone/laptop chargers handle 100–240V — check the brick.
