# Money, cards, ATMs, and VAT refund

Currency: **Euro (€, EUR)**. Card payments work almost everywhere, including small bars and bakeries. Cash is still useful for tips, markets, and a handful of older corner shops.

## Cards & contactless

- **Contactless tap (NFC) works almost universally** — Visa, Mastercard, Amex (less so), Apple Pay, Google Pay.
- Even small bars and *forns* (bakeries) accept card. A "cash only" sign is unusual outside markets, very small kiosks, and the occasional taxi (most taxis are required to accept card and almost all do).
- **PIN required for amounts over €50** [verify] approx. Contactless usually up to €50, then chip-and-PIN.
- **Amex acceptance is patchier** than Visa/MC — assume yes at hotels and bigger restaurants, maybe at small bars. Always carry a Visa/MC as backup.
- **No tipping prompt on card terminals.** Tip cash if you tip (see `tipping.md`).

## Currency-conversion trap: DCC

When you pay by card or withdraw cash, you may be asked:

> "Would you like to be charged in EUR or in your home currency (GBP/USD/etc.)?"

**Always choose EUR.**

- If you choose your home currency, the merchant's bank applies a **Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC)** — typically **3–13% worse** than your own bank's rate, plus fees.
- The screen sometimes makes "without conversion" sound risky ("No guaranteed exchange rate") — ignore the framing.
- Same rule at restaurants, hotels, shops, and especially ATMs.
- Rule: **always pay in the local currency (EUR)**, regardless of what your home card is denominated in.

## ATMs (caixers automàtics)

Two categories — choose carefully:

### Good: bank ATMs

These charge **lower fees and offer real interbank rates**:

- **CaixaBank** (blue star logo) — by far the most ATMs across Barcelona.
- **BBVA** (blue, square logo).
- **Banc Sabadell** (red dot logo).
- **Santander** (red flame logo).
- **Bankinter** (orange).

Typical fee from these: **€0–€3** depending on your home bank's agreement and amount. Some refuse foreign cards or apply a fixed €5 fee — try a different bank if the amount looks wrong.

### Avoid: Euronet and other independent ATMs

**Euronet** (yellow-and-blue / purple machines) — installed in tourist zones, hotels, metro entrances, on La Rambla:

- High withdrawal fees (€3.95–€6+ per transaction [verify]).
- Aggressive **DCC trap** that adds another 10–13%.
- Withdrawing €200 can cost you €25+ in combined fees and bad rates — equivalent to a full restaurant bill burned.

**Other independent ATMs to avoid:** Cardtronics, "Cashzone", any standalone machine without a clear bank brand. **Travelex** counters at airports and stations also offer poor rates.

### ATM tactics

- Use ATMs **inside or attached to a bank branch** when possible — safer from skimmers and from being scammed.
- Avoid ATMs on **La Rambla** specifically — disproportionately Euronet or skimmer-targeted.
- Withdraw larger amounts less often to reduce per-withdrawal fees.
- Shield the PIN pad with your other hand.
- **Always decline DCC / "pay in your currency"** — pick **EUR / "without conversion"**.
- A small wobble on the card slot can indicate a skimmer — try another machine.

## Cash usage

- ATMs dispense €50, €20, sometimes €10 notes. €100 / €200 / €500 are rare; **don't try to pay with a €500 note anywhere** — most places refuse.
- Carry small notes and coins for small purchases (€2 coffees, market stalls).
- Cash is useful for: market stalls, taxi tips, small bars in non-tourist neighbourhoods, splitting bills, tipping.
- It's safe to carry €100–€200 in cash; just split it across pockets/bags. (See `safety.md` for pickpocket precautions.)

## Currency exchange

- **Avoid airport exchange booths and street-front exchanges on La Rambla** ("0% commission" usually means a brutal exchange rate instead).
- Better: withdraw EUR from a bank ATM on arrival, or bring a multi-currency card (**Revolut, Wise, Monzo, N26**) loaded with EUR.
- Spending on a fee-free debit/credit card almost always beats cash exchange.

## Best card setups for visitors

- **Revolut / Wise / Monzo / Chase UK / Charles Schwab (US)** — low or no FX fee, real interbank rate, in-app freeze if stolen.
- A second card from a different network (Visa + Mastercard) as backup.
- Notify your home bank of travel if your card might block on first foreign transaction.

## VAT (IVA) refund — for non-EU residents

Spain's standard VAT is **21%**, on most goods (some food/books at lower rates). Non-EU residents can reclaim part of it on goods leaving the EU.

### Who qualifies

- You **live outside the EU** (passport / residence card proof).
- You're **taking the goods out of the EU within 3 months**.
- Goods are for **personal use** (not commercial quantities).

### How much you actually get back

- Headline: 21%. **Net refund typically 12–16%** after the refund operator's commission.

### Minimum amount

- **Spain abolished the minimum spend** — there is **no minimum receipt** to claim VAT refund [verify, post-2018 rule and confirmed onwards]. (You may have heard of the old €90.16 / €175 threshold — gone in Spain.)
- The €100.01 figure you may see in older guides was specific to a former threshold and no longer applies in Spain.

### Process at point of sale

1. Buy from a shop that participates in **Tax Free Shopping** (almost all major retailers do — look for *Tax Free* / *DIVA* logos: Global Blue, Planet, Travel Tax Free, Innova Taxfree).
2. Ask for a **"factura DIVA"** at checkout. Show your passport.
3. The shop issues an electronic DIVA form linked to your purchase. Keep the receipt and the form.

### At El Prat airport (BCN) on departure

1. **Before check-in if your goods go in checked luggage; after security if cabin-only.**
2. Find a **DIVA self-service kiosk** (located airside and landside at T1 and T2). Scan the barcode on each DIVA form / receipt.
3. If the kiosk gives a **green screen → validated**, no customs queue needed.
4. **Red screen** → go to the **customs / Aduana** desk to have officials verify the goods physically. They may want to see the items.
5. Once validated, collect your refund:
   - **Cash counter** (Global Blue, Planet) at the airport — minus a higher commission.
   - **To original card / bank transfer** — better rate, takes 1–4 weeks.

### Tips

- **Validate before you leave the EU** — if Barcelona is not your final EU stop (e.g. you connect through Paris on to the US), validate at the **last EU airport**.
- Keep purchases accessible — customs may want to inspect.
- Track refund online with the operator's reference number.
- Don't lose the receipts and DIVA forms; without them, no refund.

## Other money tips

- **Receipts (*tiquet* / *rebut* / *factura*)** — ask for one; useful for refunds, claims, and to verify the bill. Required for VAT refund.
- **Tax included** — all displayed prices in shops and restaurants include VAT. The price you see is the price you pay (plus optional tip).
- **Cover charges (*cobert*)** at restaurants are normal in some places — small fee for bread/setting, listed on the menu.
- **Bank scams** — your bank will never SMS asking you to "confirm a transaction" with a link. Block + report any such message.
