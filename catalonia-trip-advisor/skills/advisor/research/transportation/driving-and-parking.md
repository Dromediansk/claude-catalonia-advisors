# Driving and parking

## Should you rent a car?

| Scenario | Rent? |
|---|---|
| Barcelona city only | **No.** Parking is hard, expensive, LEZ restrictions. Metro/bus/taxi are faster. |
| Day trips to Sitges, Tarragona, Girona, Montserrat | **No.** Train (Rodalies/AVE) is faster and cheaper. |
| **Costa Brava** (Cadaqués, Cap de Creus, Begur, coves) | **Yes.** Bus/train coverage is poor; you'll waste a day waiting. |
| **Pyrenees** (Vall de Núria, Cerdanya, Andorra) | **Yes.** |
| **Penedès** wine country (Vilafranca, Sant Sadurní) | Maybe. Train gets you there; car gets you between cellars. |
| **Priorat / Montsant** wine country | **Yes.** |

## Zona de Baixes Emissions (ZBE / LEZ)

Barcelona has a **Low Emission Zone** inside the ring roads (Rondes).

- **Hours**: 07:00–20:00 **Monday–Friday** (weekends OK) `[verify]`.
- Requires a Spanish **DGT environmental sticker** (or registration for foreign vehicles).
- Excluded vehicles permanently banned: petrol cars registered before ~2006, diesel before ~2014 `[verify]`.
- **Fine**: €200 `[verify]`.
- **Foreign cars**: must register at `zberegistre.ambmobilitat.cat` **before** entering. Verification can take up to 15 days — do this before your trip.
- **Rentals from major agencies** (Hertz, Avis, Europcar, Sixt) are compliant out of the box. Cheap/independent agencies may not be — ask.

## Parking in Barcelona

| Zone | What |
|---|---|
| **Blue zone (Àrea Blava)** | Paid public street parking, ~€1–4/h `[verify]`, max 1–4 h |
| **Green zone (Àrea Verda)** | Residents priority; tourists pay much more `[verify]` and limited time |
| **Yellow zone** | Loading/no public parking |
| **No marking** | Usually OK but watch signs |

**Hotel parking**: typically €25–40/night `[verify]`. Book ahead, especially in the city centre.

### Parking apps & garages

- **ApparkB** — official AMB app for blue/green zones.
- **SabaParking** — Saba garage network (Pl. Catalunya, BAMSA garages).
- **BSM** — Barcelona Serveis Municipals (city-owned garages, cheaper).
- **Parclick**, **EasyPark**, **ElParking** — third-party booking platforms (often cheaper than walk-up).

## Renting a car

### Where to pick up

- **El Prat Airport** — best selection, multi-storey rental centre at T1; T2 has shuttle to T1 rentals.
- **Estació de Sants** — Hertz, Europcar, Sixt, Avis offices.
- **City centre** offices — pricier; airport is cheaper even with cab to/from.

### Practical notes

- **Manual transmission dominates Europe.** Request **automatic** explicitly and book early — they're scarce and pricier.
- **International Driving Permit (IDP)** recommended for non-EU drivers (especially US, Canada, Australia). Not always asked for but legally required.
- **Credit card** (not debit) usually required for deposit.
- **Minimum age**: usually 21+, often surcharge under 25 `[verify]`.
- **Insurance**: basic CDW has high excess (€1000–2500). Top up via the agency or with third parties like **iCarHireInsurance** / your credit card.

## Tolls

- Catalan motorways: **AP-7** (north to France / south to València) **is now free** since 2021. `[verify still applies]`
- Some tunnels remain tolled (Túnels de Vallvidrera, Túnel del Cadí).
- **ViaT** electronic tag — useful for long road trips; many rentals offer it as an add-on.

## Fuel

- **Gasolina 95 / 98** = petrol
- **Gasoil / Dièsel** = diesel
- **Hours**: most stations 24/7 on motorways; urban stations often 06:00–22:00 `[verify]`.
- Pay before or after pumping depending on the station — instructions in English on most pumps.
