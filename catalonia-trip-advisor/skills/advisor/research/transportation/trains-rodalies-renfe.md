# Trains in and out of Barcelona

Two tiers: **Rodalies de Catalunya** (commuter/regional, Renfe-operated under Catalan transfer) for day trips; **Renfe AVE/Avant** for long-distance high-speed.

## Stations

| Station (Catalan) | Used for |
|---|---|
| **Estació de Sants** (Barcelona-Sants) | Main hub: all AVE, Avant, long-distance, most Rodalies. Metro L3, L5. |
| **Passeig de Gràcia** | Central stop for many Rodalies and some AVE/Avant southbound. Metro L2, L3, L4. |
| **Estació de França** | Some R2 services, regional/medium-distance. Metro L4 (Barceloneta). |
| **El Clot–Aragó / La Sagrera** | Northern Rodalies hubs, future high-speed station. |

## Rodalies de Catalunya (commuter rail)

Zone-based fares; integrated with TMB passes in **Zone 1**.

| Line | Direction / Destination |
|---|---|
| **R1** | North coast: Maçanet–Massanes via Mataró, Calella, Blanes (transfer) |
| **R2 Nord** | North to Granollers / Maçanet; **stops at Aeroport T2** |
| **R2 Sud** | South coast: **Sitges**, Vilanova i la Geltrú, Sant Vicenç de Calders |
| **R3** | Vic, Puigcerdà, La Tor de Querol (Pyrenees) |
| **R4** | West: Penedès (Vilafranca), Manresa, Lleida |
| **R5 / R50** | Manresa via Montserrat (transfer at Monistrol or Aeri for the Montserrat funicular/cable car) |
| **R7** | Cerdanyola Universitat |
| **R8** | Martorell ↔ Granollers (orbital, skips Barcelona centre) |
| **R11** | Long Rodalies/regional north: Girona, Figueres, Portbou (border) |
| **R13 / R14** | Lleida via Tarragona inland |
| **R15 / R16 / R17** | Tarragona, south coast (R16/R17 toward Tortosa, Ulldecona) |

**Buy tickets**:
- Vending machines at stations
- **Rodalies de Catalunya** app
- Hola Barcelona card covers Zone 1 (including airport R2)

## Renfe long-distance

| Train type | What it is |
|---|---|
| **AVE** | High-speed (Alta Velocidad Española), up to 310 km/h |
| **Avlo** | Renfe's low-cost AVE — same speeds, smaller seats, fewer extras |

### Competing high-speed operators on Madrid route

- **Renfe AVE/Avlo**
- **Iryo** (Italian–Spanish JV, all-business-style)
- **Ouigo** (low-cost SNCF)

All three operate Barcelona Sants ↔ Madrid; compare on Trainline/Omio.

## Booking

- **renfe.com** — official, often slow and prone to non-EU card rejections. Use a VPN to Spain if it misbehaves `[verify]`.
- **Trainline** or **Omio** — friendlier UIs, small booking fee, accept all cards.
- **Rodalies app** — only for Rodalies, not long-distance.
- Book AVE/Iryo/Ouigo **as early as possible** — dynamic pricing; weeks-out prices can be 1/3 of walk-up.

## Practical tips

- **At Sants**: security scan on luggage for AVE; arrive ~30 min early.
- Rodalies has **no luggage scan** — turn up 5 min before.
- Watch for **Rodalies disruptions**: the network has chronic reliability issues — check Twitter/X @rodalies and the Rodalies app for live status before heading out.
- For **Montserrat**: R5 → Monistrol de Montserrat (then rack railway/cremallera) OR Aeri de Montserrat (then cable car). The "Trans Montserrat" combo ticket bundles train + funicular + cable car.
