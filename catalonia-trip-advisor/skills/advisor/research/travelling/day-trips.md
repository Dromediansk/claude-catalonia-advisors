# Day Trips from Barcelona

Catalan place names first; rail/bus operators noted by name. Departure station matters: confirm before sending a tourist out.

## Montserrat
- Get there: FGC line R5 from Plaça d'Espanya (FGC station, not the metro L1/L3 platforms) to either:
  - **Aeri de Montserrat** stop + cable car (Aeri) — 5-minute aerial ride, panoramic, can feel exposed.
  - **Monistrol de Montserrat** stop + Cremallera rack railway — ~17 min, calmer, runs in bad weather when cable car doesn't.
- Total journey from city centre: ~1h15–1h30.
- Frequency: R5 hourly in 2026 [verify].
- What to do: Benedictine monastery, Black Madonna (La Moreneta) — queue can be long, Escolania boys' choir performs Mon–Fri ~13:00 and Sun ~12:00 in school term (no performances during summer holidays) [verify]. Sant Joan funicular up for ridge hikes; Santa Cova funicular down for the chapel walk.
- Recommended length: half-day (5h door-to-door) for monastery + one funicular; full day if you want a serious hike.
- When to go: weekday mornings. Spring and autumn ideal — mountain stays cool when Barcelona is hot.
- When NOT to go: weekends (queues for cable car and Black Madonna), windy days (cable car closes), Christmas/Easter weeks (overwhelmed).
- Tickets: combined "Trans Montserrat" ~€50 (train + Cremallera/Aeri + both funiculars + metro day pass) or "Tot Montserrat" ~€71.50 (adds museum + lunch buffet) [verify]. Buy via cremallerademontserrat.cat or at Pl. Espanya FGC ticket office.

## Girona
- Get there:
  - **AVE / Avant high-speed**: from Barcelona Sants to Girona station, ~38 min fastest. Book in advance for cheapest fares — same-day at the kiosk is roughly double. Online via renfe.com or trainline.
  - **Rodalies R11** (regional): cheaper (~€9 each way), no booking needed, but ~1h20.
- What to do: Game of Thrones filming locations (Braavos scenes were shot here), El Call (medieval Jewish Quarter), Catedral de Girona's wide nave, Banys Àrabs (Arab baths), Onyar river houses photographed from Pont de Pedra or Pont de les Peixateries (the red Eiffel-built bridge), city walls walk.
- Recommended length: full day. Half-day works but rushed.
- When to go: spring for the temps de flors (flower festival, usually 2nd weekend of May [verify]). Year-round otherwise.
- When NOT to go: peak August midday — old town stones radiate heat.

## Costa Brava
The Costa Brava starts north of Blanes and runs to the French border. There is no direct train along most of it — you need bus or car.

### Cadaqués + Cap de Creus
- Get there: Moventis Sarfa bus from Estació del Nord (Barcelona Nord bus terminal, metro L1 Arc de Triomf). ~2h45–3h direct, fewer services off-season. Tickets at the Sarfa counter inside the station (no on-bus purchase). First bus ~07:00, last return ~19:00 [verify].
- What to do: whitewashed village around Església de Santa Maria, Dalí's house at Portlligat (book ahead — small group sizes), Cap de Creus natural park headland walks.
- Recommended length: full day from Barcelona is feasible but tight — overnight is better. Pair with Figueres if driving.
- When to go: May, June, September.
- When NOT to go: August (parking chaos, fully booked); tramuntana wind days (uncomfortable).

### Begur, Calella de Palafrugell, Tamariu, Llafranc
- Get there: Sarfa bus to Palafrugell (~2h15), local Sarfa bus onwards. Realistically needs a rental car.
- What to do: coves (calas) — Sa Tuna, Aiguablava, Sa Riera; coastal Camí de Ronda hiking path between the villages.
- Recommended length: 2-day minimum to justify the trek.
- When to go: June, September.
- When NOT to go: weekday off-season — many restaurants and shops shut.

### Tossa de Mar
- Get there: Sarfa bus from Estació del Nord, ~1h20–1h45.
- What to do: walled old town (Vila Vella), beach, castle viewpoint.
- Recommended length: full day.
- When to go: shoulder season; busy in July–August.

## Sitges
- Get there: Rodalies R2 Sud from Sants, Passeig de Gràcia, or El Clot-Aragó to Sitges, ~35–40 min. Trains every 20 min weekdays.
- What to do: 17 beaches along the curve, Església de Sant Bartomeu i Santa Tecla on the headland, Museu del Cau Ferrat (Santiago Rusiñol's house), gay nightlife on Carrer del Pecat.
- Recommended length: day trip or weekend.
- When to go: year-round mild; Carnival (Feb/March, dates shift) is the highlight; Pride (June) is huge.
- When NOT to go: mid-August Saturdays — R2 trains pack to standing-room only and beaches are mobbed.
- Price: ~€5–€7 each way [verify]. T-Casual zone 1 ticket does NOT cover Sitges (zone 3).

## Tarragona
- Get there:
  - **Avant** from Sants, ~32 min to Tarragona station (in the lower town, near the beach).
  - **AVE** stops at Camp de Tarragona station — outside the city, NOT useful unless you have onward transfer arranged.
  - **Rodalies R14 / R15 / R16 / R17**, ~1h15–1h30, cheaper.
- What to do: Roman ruins (Tarraco UNESCO site) — amphitheatre on the seafront, Roman walls, Pretori, Circ Romà; Catedral de Tarragona in the Part Alta (old town); Balcó del Mediterrani viewpoint; Platja del Miracle.
- Recommended length: full day.
- When to go: May–June, September. Santa Tecla festival (around 23 September) for human castles (castellers) and street parties.
- When NOT to go: stormy days — the Roman amphitheatre by the sea is the highlight and gets wind-lashed.

## Figueres + Dalí Theatre-Museum
- Get there:
  - **AVE** from Sants to Figueres-Vilafant, ~55 min. The AVE station is ~20-min walk from the museum (or a short bus).
  - **Rodalies R11** to Figueres central station (closer to museum) but ~2h15.
- What to do: Teatre-Museu Dalí (Dalí himself is buried beneath the stage), Castell de Sant Ferran (huge 18th-c fortress, 10-min walk).
- Recommended length: half-day for the museum; full day with the castle.
- When to go: book the Dalí museum slot ahead — sells out in summer.
- When NOT to go: Mondays off-season — museum closed [verify].
- Combine: rent a car at Figueres-Vilafant station and continue to Cadaqués / Portlligat (Dalí's house) — that's the classic loop.

## Penedès Wine Region (Vilafranca + Sant Sadurní d'Anoia)
- Get there: Rodalies R4 from Sants, Plaça de Catalunya or Passeig de Gràcia.
  - Sant Sadurní d'Anoia (~45 min) — cava heartland, base for Codorníu and Freixenet cellar visits.
  - Vilafranca del Penedès (~55 min) — wine museum (VINSEUM), Torres and Jean Leon nearby (but cellars are out of town — taxi or pre-booked transfer).
- What to do: cellar tours (book ahead — most close mid-afternoon and don't accept walk-ins), VINSEUM, tapas in Vilafranca's old town.
- Recommended length: full day.
- When to go: harvest (September) for festival energy; spring for green vineyards.
- When NOT to go: Sunday afternoons (everything shut) and August (many wineries on holiday).
