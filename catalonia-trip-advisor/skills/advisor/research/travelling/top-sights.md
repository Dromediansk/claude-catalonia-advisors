# Barcelona Top Sights

All prices and rules are subject to change. Items marked `[verify]` should be re-checked on the official site before recommending to a tourist. Catalan names first; English/Spanish in parens on first mention.

## Sagrada Família (Basílica de la Sagrada Família)
- Description: Antoni Gaudí's still-unfinished basilica, started 1882. Interior light effects are the main draw, not the exterior queues. Roof project targets 2026 (centenary of Gaudí's death) but full completion is ongoing [verify].
- Nearest metro: L2 / L5 Sagrada Família (exit puts you opposite the Nativity façade).
- Book in advance: YES — mandatory. No physical ticket office. Sells out days ahead, especially Tower access.
- Time needed: 1.5–2h (basilica + audio guide). Add 45 min for tower.
- Best time of day: first slot of the morning (around 09:00) or last 90 minutes before close — softest light through the stained glass.
- Price ballpark: basic €26, audio guide €30, guided tour €33, with tower €40. Centenary surcharge of €2–€5 applies June–December 2026 [verify].
- Booking: sagradafamilia.org/en/tickets (official site only — third-party resellers add markup).

## Park Güell (Monumental Zone)
- Description: Gaudí's hillside park-as-housing-project. Only the Monumental Zone (Plaça de la Natura, dragon staircase, hypostyle hall, mosaic bench) is ticketed; the surrounding park is free.
- Nearest metro: L3 Lesseps (15-min uphill walk) or L3 Vallcarca (with escalators — easier). Bus 24 from Plaça de Catalunya stops at the upper entrance.
- Book in advance: YES — capped at 1,400 visitors per hour in 30-min slots, sells out same-day or earlier in peak season.
- Time needed: 75–90 minutes inside Monumental Zone.
- Best time of day: first slot (09:30 summer / 10:00 shoulder) for softest light and emptier benches; late afternoon for warm tones.
- Price ballpark: adults €18, children 7–12 €13, under 7 free [verify].
- Booking: parkguell.barcelona (official). No re-entry once you exit.

## Casa Batlló
- Description: Gaudí's 2004 facade re-imagining on Passeig de Gràcia ("Block of Discord"). Skull-and-bones balconies, scale-tiled roof.
- Nearest metro: L2 / L3 / L4 Passeig de Gràcia.
- Book in advance: YES — dynamic pricing means box-office tickets cost €4–€15 more.
- Time needed: 1–1.5h with audio/AR guide.
- Best time of day: opening (08:30 "Be the First" slot) for empty rooms; evening "Magic Nights" for rooftop concerts in summer.
- Price ballpark: from €29 (Blue / general visit, online advance). Children under 12 free [verify].
- Booking: casabatllo.es.

## La Pedrera (Casa Milà)
- Description: Gaudí's wave-facade apartment building (1912). Rooftop chimneys are the highlight; attic has a structural exhibit.
- Nearest metro: L3 / L5 Diagonal, L2/L3/L4 Passeig de Gràcia.
- Book in advance: YES, especially for the night experience.
- Time needed: 1.5h day visit; 1h "Night Experience" (light + music on the roof).
- Best time of day: late afternoon for warm rooftop light; night experience for atmosphere.
- Price ballpark: day visit from €29 with audio guide; night experiences ~€39 [verify].
- Booking: lapedrera.com.

## Palau de la Música Catalana
- Description: Domènech i Montaner's 1908 modernista concert hall, UNESCO-listed. Stained-glass skylight is the showpiece. Catch a concert if you can — otherwise take a guided tour.
- Nearest metro: L1 / L4 Urquinaona.
- Book in advance: YES for guided/audio tours; concert tickets vary.
- Time needed: 1h tour. Concerts 1.5–2h.
- Best time of day: late morning when sunlight hits the skylight.
- Price ballpark: audio-guided tour €24; guided tour €24; Domènech Pass combo with Sant Pau + tapas €45 [verify].
- Booking: palaumusica.cat.

## Hospital de Sant Pau (Recinte Modernista)
- Description: Domènech i Montaner's pavilion-style hospital complex (1902–1930), UNESCO-listed, underrated. Open as a museum after the medical functions moved next door in 2009.
- Nearest metro: L5 Sant Pau | Dos de Maig. Walking distance from Sagrada Família along Avinguda de Gaudí.
- Book in advance: recommended on weekends, not strictly required weekdays.
- Time needed: 1.5–2h.
- Best time of day: morning for fewer crowds; weekend mornings to walk the underground tunnels.
- Price ballpark: general admission from €17; combined with Palau de la Música available [verify].
- Booking: santpaubarcelona.org.

## Museu Picasso
- Description: Picasso's early work (he trained in Barcelona), housed in five medieval palaces on Carrer de Montcada in El Born. Strong on his Blue Period and Las Meninas series.
- Nearest metro: L4 Jaume I.
- Book in advance: YES for paid slots and especially for free slots (released 4 days ahead at 09:00 Barcelona time, gone within hours).
- Time needed: 1.5–2h.
- Best time of day: opening hour to beat tour groups.
- Free entry windows (winter schedule, 29 Sep 2025 – 29 Mar 2026): Thursdays 16:00–19:00, every first Sunday of the month all day. Summer schedule shifts Thursday-evening free entry to Thu/Fri/Sat 19:00–21:00 [verify].
- Price ballpark: paid admission ~€14 [verify].
- Booking: museupicassobcn.cat.

## MNAC — Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya
- Description: National art museum on Montjuïc inside the Palau Nacional (1929). Romanesque mural collection (rescued from Pyrenean churches) is world-class. Rooftop terrace has one of the best free city views.
- Nearest metro: L1 / L3 Espanya, then climb up past the Magic Fountain (escalators available).
- Book in advance: recommended in peak season; not strictly required.
- Time needed: 2–3h for the highlights; longer for completists.
- Best time of day: late afternoon, then stay for the Magic Fountain after sunset (Thursday–Sunday show schedule varies seasonally [verify]).
- Free entry: every Saturday from 15:00 and the first Sunday of each month, with online reservation required.
- Price ballpark: general €12 (valid 2 days within a month from purchase) [verify].
- Booking: museunacional.cat.

## Catedral de Barcelona vs Santa Maria del Mar

### Catedral de Barcelona (La Seu)
- Description: 14th-century Gothic cathedral, gargoyles, 13 geese in the cloister (one per year of Saint Eulàlia's life). Tall and dark inside.
- Nearest metro: L4 Jaume I.
- Book in advance: not usually required.
- Time needed: 45 min including rooftop.
- Best time of day: morning. Rooftop access (lift) is the underrated bit.
- Price ballpark: ~€11 cultural visit (includes rooftop, choir, museum) [verify].

### Basílica de Santa Maria del Mar
- Description: locals' favourite. Pure Catalan Gothic, built 1329–1383 by the parish community in 54 years (uniform style). Slim columns, vast space, completely different vibe from La Seu.
- Nearest metro: L4 Jaume I, L4 Barceloneta.
- Book in advance: no for general entry; yes for the rooftop + crypt tour.
- Time needed: 30 min general; 1h with rooftop tour.
- Best time of day: late afternoon, light through the rose window.
- Price ballpark: free during worship hours; ~€10 cultural visit with rooftop add-on [verify].

## Spotify Camp Nou
- Description: FC Barcelona's stadium. Major renovation ongoing since 2023 — Barça moved back in November 2025 in a phased reopening. As of March 2026: Phase 1C, capacity ~62,652 of eventual ~105,000. Roof installation expected summer 2027. Full "Barça Stadium Tour" / museum status fluctuates with construction — confirm before recommending [verify].
- Nearest metro: L3 Palau Reial or L5 Collblanc; L9 Sud Camp Nou.
- Book in advance: YES for matches; tour availability is irregular while construction continues.
- Time needed: tour ~1.5h when running; match day plan +2h on either side.
- Best time of day: match days only at this point [verify].
- Price ballpark: tour previously ~€28–€35; matches highly variable.
- Booking: fcbarcelona.com.

## Mercat de la Boqueria (Mercat de Sant Josep)
- Description: La Rambla's covered market since 1840. Iconic but pressed by tourism; ajuntament restricts large guided groups now [verify]. Outer stalls still serve real cooks.
- Nearest metro: L3 Liceu.
- Book in advance: no — just walk in.
- Time needed: 30–60 min. Closed Sundays.
- Best time of day: 08:30–10:30 weekday mornings (real shopping); avoid 12:00–15:00 weekend crush.
- Price ballpark: free entry; juices ~€2–€3, jamón cone ~€5, counter lunch at El Quim or Pinotxo ~€25–€40.
