# Hidden Spots and Local Favourites

Places that don't show up on the first page of most guidebooks but reward a half-day each. Most are free or very cheap. Plan around opening hours — several close Mondays.

## Bunkers del Carmel / Turó de la Rovira
- What: WWII-era anti-aircraft battery (1937) at 262 m, repurposed as a panoramic platform with 360° views over the whole city, sea to mountains. Now part of the MUHBA (Barcelona History Museum) as MUHBA Turó de la Rovira.
- Where: Carrer de Marià Labèrnia, Horta-Guinardó.
- Get there: metro L4 Alfons X then bus V17 or 119 — bus drops you ~10 min walk from the top. Or taxi to the Carmel viewpoint.
- Hours: officially summer 09:00–19:30, winter 09:00–17:30 [verify]. Free.
- Catch: the area is patrolled and shut around sunset by police in 2026 to control crowds and noise — show up well before sunset, leave before closure, or you may be fined [verify].
- Best for: panoramic context before/after big sights; bring water and a layer (windy).

## Tibidabo + Temple Expiatori del Sagrat Cor
- What: highest point in the Collserola range (512 m) crowned by a neo-Gothic basilica (Sagrat Cor) with a Christ statue on top, and an old amusement park (Parc d'Atraccions del Tibidabo, opened 1899 — one of Europe's oldest).
- Where: Plaça del Tibidabo.
- Get there: FGC L7 to Av. Tibidabo, then Tramvia Blau or bus 196 to Plaça Doctor Andreu, then Funicular del Tibidabo to the top. The full sequence is part of the experience; the historic Tramvia Blau has had repeated service interruptions [verify].
- Hours: basilica generally 09:30–19:00 [verify]; amusement park open weekends + school holidays (check calendar).
- Best for: families (amusement park), photographers (sunset over the city — even better than Bunkers because higher), Sunday mass in the basilica.

## Parc del Laberint d'Horta
- What: oldest preserved park in the city (1791, neoclassical), with a hedge maze, romantic gardens, and water features.
- Where: Passeig dels Castanyers, Horta.
- Get there: metro L3 Mundet (~10 min walk).
- Hours: 10:00–sunset; closed Mondays [verify]. Small entrance fee (~€2.50) [verify], free Wednesdays and Sundays.
- Best for: shaded picnic, photo walks, escaping summer heat. Quiet even in August.

## Sant Pau del Camp
- What: oldest surviving church in Barcelona (Romanesque, founded before 1117). Small cloister with horseshoe arches, Visigothic spolia. Tucked into the back of El Raval.
- Where: Carrer de Sant Pau 101, El Raval.
- Get there: metro L3 Paral·lel or L2 Sant Antoni.
- Hours: short visiting windows, typically Mon–Sat afternoons; check the parish schedule [verify]. Donation entrance ~€5.
- Best for: anyone tired of Gothic basilicas — completely different mood and scale.

## Recinte Modernista de Sant Pau (Hospital de Sant Pau)
- What: Domènech i Montaner's pavilion-style hospital complex, UNESCO-listed, finished 1930. 12 connected modernista pavilions plus underground tunnels. Far less crowded than Sagrada Família but architecturally on par.
- Where: Carrer de Sant Quintí, Eixample (Avinguda de Gaudí connects it directly to Sagrada Família — 12-min walk).
- Get there: metro L5 Sant Pau | Dos de Maig.
- Hours: 09:30–17:00 winter, 09:30–18:30 summer [verify]. Closed some Sunday afternoons.
- Best for: pairing with Sagrada Família the same morning — same architectural era, different architect, opposite ends of the same avenue.

## Carrer de Blai (Poble-sec pintxo street)
- What: pedestrianised street lined with bars serving pintxos (small bites on toothpicks) typically €1–€2.50 each. You pile up the sticks and they count at the end. Tasca-style, loud, fun.
- Where: Carrer de Blai, Poble-sec.
- Get there: metro L2/L3 Paral·lel or L3 Poble Sec.
- Hours: most bars open ~12:00–16:00 lunch, 19:00–24:00 dinner; busiest Thursday–Saturday from 20:30.
- Pick a few: Blai Tonight, La Tasqueta de Blai, Koska Taverna. Don't sit down at the first one — walk the strip first.
- Best for: cheap dinner, group atmosphere, second-night-out spot.

## Carretera de les Aigües
- What: ~9 km level dirt path along the Collserola hillside above the city — old water-pipeline service road. Steady views over Barcelona the whole way; popular with runners, cyclists, dog-walkers (locals only — almost no tourists).
- Where: trailhead easiest at Peu del Funicular.
- Get there: FGC L7 to Peu del Funicular, then Funicular de Vallvidrera up one stop to Carretera de les Aigües station.
- Hours: anytime, free.
- Best for: morning run/walk with city view; sunset stretch from Sant Pere Màrtir.

## Mercat dels Encants (Encants Vells)
- What: city flea market under a mirrored canopy roof — antiques, second-hand books and electronics, junk treasure. The roof itself is the photo.
- Where: Plaça de les Glòries Catalanes, near Torre Glòries.
- Get there: metro L1 Glòries.
- Hours: typically Mon, Wed, Fri, Sat 09:00–17:00 [verify]; auctions early morning.
- Best for: design lovers, vintage hunters; pair with a coffee at Espai Joliu or a Torre Glòries observation deck visit.

## Refugi 307 (MUHBA)
- What: Civil War bomb shelter dug into Montjuïc's flank, ~400 m of tunnels. Guided visits only, in Catalan/Spanish/English on rotation.
- Where: Nou de la Rambla 175, Poble-sec.
- Get there: metro L3 Paral·lel.
- Hours: Sunday visits only, advance booking via MUHBA [verify].
- Best for: history-curious visitors who've ticked Gaudí off.

## Bunker Vert / Jardins de Mossèn Cinto Verdaguer + Mossèn Costa i Llobera (cactus garden)
- What: two specialised Montjuïc gardens — one Mediterranean cactus and succulents on the south-eastern slope (sea views), the other water lilies and flowers further up.
- Where: Montjuïc slopes.
- Get there: bus 150 from Plaça d'Espanya, or the Montjuïc Funicular + walk.
- Hours: dawn to dusk, free.
- Best for: free shaded escape on hot days; spectacular sea-facing cactus garden in flower mid-spring.

## Búnker de Sant Andreu / Casa de l'Aigua de Trinitat Vella
- What: lesser-known Civil War shelter and a modernista waterworks. Both run by MUHBA on a limited visit calendar.
- Best for: completists; check MUHBA's program.

## El Born CCM (Cultural Centre)
- What: the former Mercat del Born — a wrought-iron 1876 market hall now covering an excavated 1700s neighbourhood (destroyed when Felipe V's Ciutadella fortress was built). Free to walk in and look down at the dig.
- Where: Plaça Comercial, El Born.
- Get there: metro L4 Barceloneta or L4 Jaume I.
- Hours: typically Tue–Sun 10:00–20:00 [verify]; free general entry, paid for the archaeological zone.
- Best for: history fans, rain day; pairs naturally with Santa Maria del Mar next door.

## Fundació Joan Miró
- What: Miró's purpose-built museum on Montjuïc, designed by his friend Josep Lluís Sert in 1975. Lighter and quieter than MNAC.
- Where: Parc de Montjuïc.
- Get there: bus 150 or Montjuïc Funicular.
- Hours: Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00, longer in summer [verify]. Closed Mondays.
- Best for: Miró is the second name to know in Barcelona after Gaudí — and his work is far less visible than Gaudí's. Roof terrace included.
