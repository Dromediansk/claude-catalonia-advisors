# Beaches and Coast

Barcelona's coastline runs ~4.5 km within the city, north-east from the port. Beach season is roughly mid-May to early October; lifeguards on duty typically June–September [verify]. Topless sunbathing legal and common everywhere; full nudity restricted to specific zones.

## In-City Beaches (south-to-north along the coast)

### Platja de Sant Sebastià
- Vibe: closest to the port, less crowded than its neighbour.
- Metro: L4 Barceloneta.
- Length: ~660 m.
- Notes: W Hotel ("the sail") sits at the southern end; quieter than Barceloneta itself.

### Platja de la Barceloneta
- Vibe: most touristy in-city beach, surrounded by seafood restaurants and the Barceloneta grid.
- Metro: L4 Barceloneta.
- Length: ~420 m.
- Pros: easy access, lots of services (showers, kiosks, Wi-Fi), accessible boardwalks.
- Cons: very crowded weekends and any time in July–August; pickpockets on the sand — never leave a bag unattended.

### Platja del Somorrostro
- Vibe: extension of Barceloneta heading north, slightly less mobbed.
- Metro: L4 Ciutadella | Vila Olímpica.
- Notes: marker for the historic shantytown that stood here until the 1960s.

### Platja de la Nova Icària
- Vibe: family beach, calmer water, beach volleyball courts.
- Metro: L4 Ciutadella | Vila Olímpica.
- Notes: backed by the Port Olímpic marina; convenient lunch in the Vila Olímpica grid.

### Platja del Bogatell
- Vibe: locals' favourite. Cleaner, wider, less stag-party energy than Barceloneta.
- Metro: L4 Llacuna or L4 Poblenou (10-min walk).
- Length: ~700 m.
- Why pick this: same sand and water as Barceloneta but noticeably fewer tourists.

### Platja de la Mar Bella
- Vibe: alternative, with a designated clothing-optional/nudist section at the northern end. LGBTQ+-friendly chiringuito (Chiringuito BeGay area) [verify].
- Metro: L4 Poblenou or L4 Selva de Mar.
- Notes: skateboard and beach-sport areas inland.

### Platja de la Nova Mar Bella
- Vibe: residential, used by the new Diagonal Mar district.
- Metro: L4 Selva de Mar.
- Notes: backed by parks, good for runners.

### Platja del Llevant
- Vibe: northernmost city beach, opened 2006, area still developing.
- Metro: L4 Selva de Mar (15-min walk) or tram T4 Fòrum.
- Notes: dog-friendly section in low season (October–March) [verify].

## Practical In-City Beach Tips
- Pickpocketing is the #1 hazard — keep phone/wallet on you in the water, or use a waterproof neck pouch.
- Beach hawkers selling mojitos, towels, massages and sunglasses are unlicensed; politely refuse if not interested.
- Public toilets are limited; use a chiringuito (beach bar) by buying a drink.
- The metro's Barceloneta and Ciutadella | Vila Olímpica stations close earlier than the rest [verify]; check return times.
- New 2024–2025 ordinances restrict alcohol consumption on the sand outside chiringuitos [verify] — fines apply.

## Quick Coastal Escapes (under 1h by train)

### Castelldefels
- Get there: Rodalies R2 Sud from Sants/Passeig de Gràcia, ~25–30 min to Castelldefels Platja (NOT Castelldefels — the town station is inland).
- Beach: 5 km of wide sand, much less crowded than Barcelona.
- Best for: locals' summer day-out with families; kite surfing.
- When NOT to go: weekday off-season — most chiringuitos shut.

### Sitges (see day-trips.md)
- 35–40 min on R2 Sud. 17 separate beaches including the small calas south of town (Cala Morisca, Cala dels Balmins — clothing-optional). Best mid-distance escape.

### Garraf
- Get there: R2 Sud between Castelldefels and Sitges, ~30 min.
- Small fishing village with white-trim beach huts. Tiny beach, very atmospheric.

## Costa Brava Beaches (transport needed)

These require bus or car (see day-trips.md). Day trips are tight; an overnight is better.

### Calella de Palafrugell, Llafranc, Tamariu
- Three linked villages on the Camí de Ronda coastal path. Calella is the most postcard-perfect; Tamariu the smallest and calmest.
- Access: bus to Palafrugell + local connection.

### Sa Tuna, Aiguablava, Sa Riera (Begur coast)
- Small coves with crystal water, hemmed by pines. Begur town up on the hill.
- Access: car essential.

### Cala Pola, Cala Giverola (north of Tossa)
- Coves accessible by boat from Tossa or a hike.

### Cap de Creus / Cala Culip / Cala Tavellera
- Wild headland north-east of Cadaqués. No services. Hiking required.

### Playa de la Concha (Llançà), Punta Falconera (Roses)
- Northernmost Costa Brava beaches before the French border.

## Choosing Quickly
- Just want sand near the hotel: Bogatell over Barceloneta.
- Family with kids: Nova Icària.
- Naturist / LGBTQ+: Mar Bella nudist section.
- Half-day beach without leaving the metro: Bogatell + lunch at Xiringuito Escribà.
- Cleaner sand, fewer crowds, day trip: Sitges or Castelldefels.
- Postcard Mediterranean: Costa Brava — but plan the transport.
