# When to Visit Barcelona

Barcelona has a Mediterranean climate: mild winters, hot humid summers, two short shoulder seasons. Temperatures cited are typical daily highs.

## Quick Recommendation
- **Best months for first-timers**: May, June, September, early October.
- **Avoid if you can**: mid-July through August (heat, humidity, locals on holiday, some places shut).
- **Underrated**: late February through March (cool, dry, cheap, uncrowded — but some beach activity off).

## Season by Season

### Winter (December – February)
- Weather: 13–16 °C daytime, 5–9 °C at night. Occasional rain. Rarely freezes. Sunny days are common.
- Crowds: lowest of the year except Christmas/New Year and the bridge weekends.
- Prices: cheapest flights and hotels (except Christmas–6 January).
- Daylight: ~9.5 hours in late December.
- Reduced hours: some smaller attractions cut Mondays + close earlier; chiringuitos shut.
- Festivals:
  - **Nadal / Christmas markets**: Fira de Santa Llúcia at the Cathedral (1 Dec – 23 Dec [verify]).
  - **Cap d'Any (New Year)**: Avinguda Maria Cristina fountain show.
  - **Cavalcada de Reis (Three Kings Parade)**: 5 January evening — bigger than Christmas Eve here.
  - **Festes de Santa Eulàlia**: around 12 February — the city's "winter Mercè" with castellers and parades.
- Trade-offs: beach off the table; Park Güell and Sagrada Família still excellent (less queue).

### Spring (March – May)
- Weather: March 16–18 °C climbing to May 22–24 °C. April can be rainy.
- Crowds: building from April; Easter week is intense.
- Prices: mid; spike at Easter and around Mobile World Congress (late February / early March) [verify].
- Daylight: 11–14 hours.
- Festivals:
  - **Sant Jordi (23 April)**: books and roses across the city — La Rambla and Passeig de Gràcia transform. Catalonia's most beloved day. Stay overnight.
  - **Semana Santa / Holy Week**: processions but smaller than in Andalucía.
  - **Primavera Sound**: typically late May / early June at Parc del Fòrum [verify dates].
- When NOT to go: Easter week if you dislike crowds; Mobile World Congress for hotel scarcity.

### Summer (June – August)
- Weather: 26–29 °C with humidity that makes it feel hotter. Sea temperature 22–26 °C from June. Nights stay 19–23 °C.
- Crowds: peak. La Rambla, Park Güell, Sagrada Família reach capacity early.
- Prices: peak. Hotels can double from May rates.
- Daylight: up to 15 hours in late June.
- Festivals:
  - **Sónar** (mid-June): electronic music.
  - **Sant Joan (23–24 June)**: bonfires on the beach, fireworks across the city, biggest night-out of the year.
  - **Grec Festival** (July): performing arts on Montjuïc.
  - **Festa Major de Gràcia** (mid-August, around 15 August): decorated streets and free concerts in Gràcia.
  - **Festa Major de Sants** (late August): similar in Sants.
- August specifics:
  - Locals leave the city; many small restaurants and shops close for 2–3 weeks (often the whole month).
  - Big tourist attractions stay open but service quality dips.
  - Beaches packed; metro fills with travellers more than commuters.
  - Hottest stretch usually first two weeks; heatwaves above 35 °C are now annual.
- Trade-offs: best for beach + nightlife, worst for everything else.

### Autumn (September – November)
- Weather: September still 25 °C, October 21–23 °C, November 16–18 °C. Sea swimmable through early October.
- Crowds: September matches July; tapering rapidly from mid-October.
- Prices: drop from mid-October.
- Daylight: 11–13 hours into October, then shrinks.
- Festivals:
  - **La Diada Nacional de Catalunya (11 September)**: Catalan national day — large political demonstrations; mostly peaceful but expect closures and metro disruptions.
  - **La Mercè (around 24 September)**: Barcelona's biggest festival, 4–5 days of free concerts, castellers (human towers), correfocs (fire-runs), gegants. The single best week to be in town if you can handle crowds.
  - **48h Open House BCN** (October): free access to ~150 buildings normally closed.
- October–November notes: weather can swing — pack layers and a rain shell.
- November: a sweet spot — cheap, mild, museums uncrowded.

## Picking by Trip Type
- First visit, sightseeing focus: mid-May to mid-June, or mid-September to mid-October.
- Beach + nightlife: late June, early July, or September (still warm sea).
- Cheap city break: November or late January.
- Foodie deep-dive: September–October (truffle, mushrooms, calçots in February).
- Family with school-age kids: Easter or late June.
- Avoid for everyone: 1–25 August unless you specifically want festa atmosphere.
