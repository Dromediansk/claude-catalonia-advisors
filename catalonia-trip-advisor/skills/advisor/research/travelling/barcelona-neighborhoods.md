# Barcelona Neighborhoods (Barris)

Barcelona is administratively divided into 10 districts and 73 barris. Tourists mostly stay within Ciutat Vella, Eixample, and Gràcia. Catalan names are used first; English/Spanish equivalents follow on first mention.

## Ciutat Vella (Old City)

### Barri Gòtic (Barrio Gótico / Gothic Quarter)
- Vibe: medieval stone-canyon streets, heavily touristy, atmospheric at dawn.
- What's there: Catedral de Barcelona, Plaça del Rei, Plaça Sant Jaume (city hall + Generalitat), Plaça Reial, Roman wall fragments, Carrer del Bisbe bridge.
- Metro: L3 Liceu, L4 Jaume I, L1/L3 Plaça de Catalunya.
- When to visit: early morning (before 10:00) or evening for fewer crowds.
- Who it suits: first-timers, history buffs, short stays. Avoid as accommodation if you want quiet sleep.

### El Born (La Ribera)
- Vibe: chic, design-y, packed with tapas bars and cocktail spots.
- What's there: Museu Picasso, Basílica de Santa Maria del Mar, Mercat del Born (cultural centre, not a food market), Passeig del Born, Parc de la Ciutadella eastern edge.
- Metro: L4 Jaume I, L4 Barceloneta.
- When to visit: evenings for dinner and drinks; Sunday mornings for Santa Maria del Mar mass.
- Who it suits: couples, foodies, second-time visitors.

### El Raval
- Vibe: gritty-cool, multicultural, sharp edges next to art galleries.
- What's there: MACBA (contemporary art), CCCB, Rambla del Raval, Sant Pau del Camp (oldest church), Filmoteca, Boqueria's back entrance.
- Metro: L3 Liceu, L2 Sant Antoni, L1/L2 Universitat.
- When to visit: daytime for culture; lower Raval (south of Carrer de l'Hospital) gets sketchy late at night — watch your phone and bag.
- Who it suits: independent travellers, art crowd, budget eaters. Not ideal for first-time families.

### Barceloneta
- Vibe: former fishermen's quarter, tight grid, beach on the doorstep.
- What's there: Platja de la Barceloneta, seafood restaurants on Carrer de l'Almirall Cervera and Passeig Joan de Borbó, Mercat de la Barceloneta, Torre del Rellotge.
- Metro: L4 Barceloneta.
- When to visit: lunch for paella and seafood; weekday beach mornings (weekends are mobbed).
- Who it suits: beach lovers on a short trip. Apartments here have heavy anti-tourist sentiment — be discreet with luggage.

## Eixample (L'Eixample)

### Dreta de l'Eixample
- Vibe: grid-pattern bourgeoisie, Modernisme showcase, upscale shopping.
- What's there: Sagrada Família, Casa Batlló, La Pedrera (Casa Milà), Passeig de Gràcia (shops + Manzana de la Discòrdia block), Hospital de Sant Pau.
- Metro: L2/L5 Sagrada Família, L3/L5 Diagonal, L2/L3/L4 Passeig de Gràcia.
- When to visit: any time; Passeig de Gràcia is best on weekday mornings.
- Who it suits: first-timers, architecture lovers, mid-to-high budget.

### Esquerra de l'Eixample
- Vibe: residential, calmer, less touristy, gay-friendly around "Gaixample" (around Carrer de Muntaner/Casanova).
- What's there: Universitat de Barcelona historic building, Mercat del Ninot, Arenas de Barcelona (former bullring, now mall), edge of Sant Antoni.
- Metro: L1 Urgell, L5 Hospital Clínic, L3/L5 Diagonal.
- When to visit: stay here for a more local feel; eat where the office workers eat.
- Who it suits: repeat visitors, longer stays, those who want quiet at night.

## Gràcia
- Vibe: bohemian, village-within-the-city, small plaças full of locals at apéro.
- What's there: Plaça del Sol, Plaça de la Vila de Gràcia, Plaça del Diamant, Mercat de l'Abaceria (under renovation [verify]), independent shops on Carrer Verdi, Park Güell uphill.
- Metro: L3 Fontana, L3 Lesseps, L4 Joanic, L6/L7 Gràcia (FGC).
- When to visit: evenings for tapas and vermouth; mid-August for Festa Major de Gràcia (decorated streets).
- Who it suits: returners, slow travellers, anyone tired of La Rambla.

## Poblenou (Sant Martí district)
- Vibe: ex-industrial, now design district + tech offices (22@), beach access.
- What's there: Rambla del Poblenou, Platja del Bogatell and Platja de la Nova Mar Bella, Palo Alto market (monthly), former factories repurposed into studios, Torre Glòries.
- Metro: L4 Poblenou, L4 Llacuna, L1 Glòries.
- When to visit: warm-weather afternoons for the beach + Rambla del Poblenou stroll.
- Who it suits: digital nomads, families wanting beach without Barceloneta crowds.

## Sant Antoni
- Vibe: gentrified-cool, foodie nucleus, still has neighbourhood feel.
- What's there: Mercat de Sant Antoni (renovated 2018, book exchange Sundays), Carrer del Parlament (vermouth bars), Carrer de Tamarit.
- Metro: L2 Sant Antoni, L1/L2 Universitat.
- When to visit: Sunday morning for the book market; evenings for vermouth.
- Who it suits: foodies, returners, people staying 4+ nights.

## Sants-Montjuïc
- Vibe: transport hub (Sants) + green mountain (Montjuïc) — two different worlds.
- What's there: Estació de Sants (main train station), MNAC, Font Màgica (Magic Fountain), Poble Espanyol, Fundació Joan Miró, Castell de Montjuïc, Olympic Ring, Jardins de Mossèn Costa i Llobera (cactus garden), Mercat de Sants.
- Metro: L1/L3/L5 Sants Estació, L1/L3 Espanya, L2/L3 Paral·lel (then funicular up Montjuïc).
- When to visit: Montjuïc on clear days; Magic Fountain shows Thursday–Sunday evenings (schedule varies seasonally) [verify].
- Who it suits: stay in Sants only for early train departures; visit Montjuïc as a half-day trip.

## Sarrià-Sant Gervasi / Tibidabo
- Vibe: uphill, residential and wealthy, panoramic views, low tourist density.
- What's there: Tibidabo amusement park, Temple Expiatori del Sagrat Cor, Torre de Collserola, CosmoCaixa science museum, old Sarrià village core.
- Metro: FGC L7 Av. Tibidabo (then Tramvia Blau when running [verify] or bus 196), FGC L6 Reina Elisenda for Sarrià.
- When to visit: clear day for views; Tibidabo amusement park weekends + summer.
- Who it suits: families, second-time visitors, view-seekers.

## Horta-Guinardó
- Vibe: working-class hillside, off the tourist trail except for one sunset spot.
- What's there: Bunkers del Carmel / Turó de la Rovira (panoramic view), Parc del Laberint d'Horta (oldest park in the city), Hospital de Sant Pau (technically Eixample border).
- Metro: L4 Alfons X (then bus 119 to Bunkers), L5 Horta.
- When to visit: late afternoon for Bunkers — but note the area is closed by police around sunset in 2026 [verify].
- Who it suits: hikers, photographers, repeat visitors who've done the obvious sights.

## Poble-sec
- Vibe: foot of Montjuïc, tapas street culture, working-class roots.
- What's there: Carrer de Blai (pintxo street), Mercat de les Flors, Teatre Grec, Refugi 307 (Civil War shelter).
- Metro: L3 Poble Sec, L2/L3 Paral·lel.
- When to visit: evenings, especially Thursday–Saturday on Carrer de Blai.
- Who it suits: budget eaters, nightlife seekers.
