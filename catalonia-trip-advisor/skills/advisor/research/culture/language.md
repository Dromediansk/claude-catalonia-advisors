# Language

## The basics

- **Catalan** (*català*) and **Spanish** (*castellà* / *castellano*) are both co-official in Catalonia.
- Catalan is the default language of the regional government, public schools, most street signs, public transport announcements, and many menus.
- Spanish is **universally understood and spoken** — you will never be stranded by not speaking Catalan.
- English varies widely: common in central Barcelona, tourist neighborhoods, and among younger people; patchier in Gràcia bars, neighborhood shops, and outside Barcelona.
- Catalan is **not a dialect of Spanish**. It's a separate Romance language, closer to Occitan / southern French than to Castilian. Calling it a dialect is a minor insult.

## What language to start with

- A greeting in Catalan (*bon dia*) is the friendliest opener anywhere in Catalonia. Locals appreciate the effort even when they reply in Spanish or English.
- After the greeting, switch to Spanish or English if you don't speak Catalan — nobody expects tourists to be fluent.
- In Barcelona's tourist core (Las Ramblas, Sagrada Família, Gothic Quarter) staff often default to Spanish or English with foreigners. Outside the center, Catalan first is more common.

## Reading a Catalan menu

Common words you'll see:

- *Pa* — bread. *Pa amb tomàquet* = bread rubbed with tomato (a Catalan staple, not a topping).
- *Truita* — **omelette**, not trout. *Truita de patates* = potato omelette (Spanish *tortilla*).
- *Peix* — fish. *Bacallà* = salt cod. *Lluç* = hake. *Rap* = monkfish.
- *Carn* — meat. *Vedella* = beef/veal. *Xai* / *be* = lamb. *Porc* = pork. *Pollastre* = chicken.
- *Amanida* — salad.
- *Arròs* — rice (*arròs negre* = squid-ink rice; *arròs a la cassola* = baked rice).
- *Fideus* — short noodles (basis of *fideuà*).
- *Verdures* / *hortalisses* — vegetables.
- *Bolets* — mushrooms (a big deal in autumn).
- *Embotits* — cured meats / charcuterie. *Fuet*, *botifarra*, *llonganissa*.
- *Postres* — dessert. *Mel i mató* = fresh cheese with honey. *Crema catalana* = burnt-cream custard.
- *Aigua amb gas* / *sense gas* — sparkling / still water.
- *De la casa* — house specialty.
- *Per a compartir* — to share.

## False friends (Catalan vs Spanish)

- *Truita* (CAT, omelette) ≠ *trucha* (SP, trout).
- *Tapes* (CAT) = *tapas* (SP). Same thing.
- *Entrepà* (CAT) = *bocadillo* (SP) = baguette sandwich.

## What about Spanish phrases?

- Spanish works everywhere. Default to *hola*, *gracias*, *por favor*, *la cuenta* if you already know them — nobody will be offended.
- The polite move is: Catalan greeting → continue in whatever you actually speak.
