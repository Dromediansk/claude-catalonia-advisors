# Football

## The two clubs

- **FC Barcelona** (***Barça***) — La Liga, founded 1899, colors *blaugrana* (deep blue and garnet).
- **RCD Espanyol** (***Espanyol***) — historically Spain's "other" Barcelona club, founded 1900, blue and white. Plays at **RCDE Stadium** in Cornellà-El Prat. [verify current league — promoted/relegated status]
- Don't mix them up in casual conversation. The rivalry (***el derbi barceloní***) is real but Barça dominates attention.

## ***Més que un club*** — more than a club

- FC Barcelona's motto since the 1960s. Reflects the club's role as a vehicle for Catalan identity, especially under the Franco dictatorship, when Catalan symbols were banned and Camp Nou was one of the few places people could speak Catalan publicly.
- **El Clásico** (vs Real Madrid) is loaded with this history — Madrid as the "regime club" is mostly retired framing, but the political charge hasn't fully gone.
- Don't be surprised by *senyeres* and political chants in the stadium.

## Spotify Camp Nou — renovation status (May 2026)

The stadium is mid-renovation under the *Espai Barça* project, eventually expanding capacity to ~105,000.

- **Returned to use** during the 2025–26 season. First competitive match back: **22 November 2025**, vs Athletic Club (4–0 win).
- **Current capacity (as of March 2026)**: **~62,652 seats** under the Phase 1C occupancy license. Phase 1B previously allowed ~45,401. Capacity has been expanding in stages as more sections get certified. [verify current available capacity at time of booking]
- **Third tier and 50,000 m² roof** still under construction; full completion targeted for **mid-2026** (around June 2026). [verify — slips have happened before]
- **Tour and museum**: the official Camp Nou tour has been reorganized during works. Check the FC Barcelona official site for what's currently open. [verify availability]
- While Camp Nou was fully closed (2023–25 seasons), Barça played at **Estadi Olímpic Lluís Companys** on Montjuïc and at the **Estadi Johan Cruyff** (training ground). ## Buying tickets — only safe routes

- **Official only**:
  - **fcbarcelona.com** (or the official app).
  - **Authorized resale via the official Seient Lliure / Spotify Seat Plus platform** — Barça's own system where season-ticket holders release their seats. This is the legitimate secondary market.
- **For RCD Espanyol**: rcdespanyol.com.
- **Do NOT buy from**:
  - Street touts around the stadium or in the Gothic Quarter.
  - Random third-party resale sites with unverifiable seat numbers — scams and counterfeit QR codes are common.
  - "Hospitality packages" offered by unknown agents on social media.
- Tickets often release **2–3 weeks before** a match. El Clásico and Champions League knockouts go fast — check listings the moment they open.
- Bring **ID matching the ticket** for many entries — the club has run nominative ticketing campaigns to crack down on resale.

## Matchday practical tips

- **Getting there (Camp Nou)**: Metro L3 (green) to *Palau Reial* or *Les Corts*; L5 (blue) to *Collblanc* or *Badal*. Allow 20–30 minutes to walk from metro to your gate. Trains are packed for 1–2 hours after the final whistle.
- **Getting there (Montjuïc, when used)**: Metro L1 to *Espanya* + 15-min walk uphill, or shuttle buses on matchdays.
- **Getting there (RCDE Stadium)**: Metro L5 to *Cornellà Centre*, then ~15 min walk; or FGC line S33.
- **Arrive early** — security checks slow down entry. Most stadiums close gates near kickoff.
- **What you can / can't bring**: no large bags, no professional cameras, no flares, no glass. Check the official restrictions page before going.
- **Food/drink inside**: limited and overpriced; eat before. Beer inside is alcohol-free by Spanish stadium law.
- **Weather**: Camp Nou has very limited cover even after renovation. Bring a poncho if rain is forecast — umbrellas may be refused at security.

## Away-fan etiquette

- Wearing Barça colors in central Barcelona is fine any day. Wearing Madrid or Espanyol colors near Camp Nou on matchday — not recommended.
- The away end at Camp Nou is segregated and escorted in/out. If you're an away fan, sit there, not in the home sections.
- Standing and singing all match is normal in some sections; sitting quiet is normal in others. Read the room.
- Recording video is fine; flash photography during play is discouraged.

## Local football culture beyond match tickets

- ***Penyes*** — official Barça fan clubs, found in many neighborhoods and abroad. The closest you'll get to a local supporters' pub.
- The **FC Barcelona Museum** is one of the most-visited museums in Spain — tour usually includes pitchside, dressing rooms, trophy room. [verify what's open during renovation]
- ***La Masia*** — Barça's famed youth academy (now relocated to the training-ground complex). Not normally open to the public.
